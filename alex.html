tldr:   I’m a robotics engineer turned product designer working in NYC. I combine human-centered design with advanced technology to solve problems and help people. I’m currently teaching UX Design at General Assembly while I plan my next move.

Who I am

I’m an interdisciplinary product designer living and working in NYC.

  • I graduated from RISD in 2012, where I studied Industrial Design.
  • After that, I designed some really cool shit at Honeybee Robotics.
  • I switched from physical to digital products in 2015 after learning to code.
  • I am currently teaching UX Design at General Assembly in NYC.

What I do

At the most basic level, I combine human-centered design with advanced technology to solve real problems for real people. More specifically I:

  • Research - to understand complexity & navigate ambiguity
  • Observe & empathize with users
  • Identify & define problems to solve
  • Ideate & explore potential solutions
  • Design advanced digital & physical systems that solve those problems
  • Test those systems to validate usability & utility

When I am not designing stuff, you can find me: throwing darts, throwing pottery, throwing parties & throwing my phone against the wall when I read the news.

Why I do it

  1. Improvement is a compulsion. I spend most of my waking hours thinking about how to make the things around me better. Sometimes those things are physical objects, sometimes they’re digital applications, & sometimes they’re geopolitical systems of governance. Whatever it happens to be, I really only have two modes of thinking about it: ‘how does it work?’ & ‘how could it work better?’
    *yes, this occasionally interferes with my personal relationships
  2. I am one of those romantic designers who sincerely believes that design & design thinking will save the world. I believe that the practices and principles of great design are applicable to all human endeavors and are an incredible tool for enacting positive change at every conceivable scale. My mission is to help people; design is how I help.

I realize that all might sound broad & romantic but it’s accurate ¯\_(ツ)_/¯

How I do it

More important than any of the tools I use in my design practice is the process itself. Although, my process is evolving and varies given different circumstances, it boils down to a couple simple elements: understanding through empathy, exploration through making, & validation through testing. I am a big fan of designing the right thing before designing the thing right.

Tools

I use a lot of different tools at every stage of my design process. Some of those tools are analog like paper & post-its, others are software like Sketch & Solidworks, but at the end of the day they’re all just a means to deliver solutions that solve problems.

Digital Tools: Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, Sketch, InVision, & Flinto.
Physical Tools: Solidworks, Autodesk Inventor, & Blender.
Dev Tools: HTML, CSS, & JavaScript.
